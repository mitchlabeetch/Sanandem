### About the Data

#### What This Data Represents
- **Crowdsourced experiences**: Anonymized self-reported medication effects
- **Observational data**: No controlled experimental conditions
- **Unverified reports**: Individual experiences, not clinically validated
- **Statistical patterns**: Trends that may warrant further research

#### What This Data Does NOT Represent
- **Not clinical trials**: No controlled methodology or peer review
- **Not causation**: Correlations do not prove cause-and-effect
- **Not comprehensive**: May not capture all possible effects
- **Not prescriptive**: Cannot tell you what will happen to you individually

### Limitations of Crowdsourced Data

1. **Self-Selection Bias**: People with strong reactions (positive or negative) are more likely to report
2. **Recall Bias**: People may misremember or misattribute effects
3. **Confounding Variables**: Multiple factors may contribute to reported effects
4. **No Medical Verification**: Reports are not confirmed by healthcare professionals
5. **Sample Limitations**: May not represent all populations or conditions
6. **Temporal Changes**: Medication formulations and usage patterns change over time

### Official Reporting Channels

If you experience an adverse drug reaction, report it to:

**United States:**
- FDA MedWatch: https://www.fda.gov/safety/medwatch
- Call: 1-800-FDA-1088

**European Union:**
- EudraVigilance: https://www.ema.europa.eu/en
- Contact your national medicines agency

**United Kingdom:**
- Yellow Card Scheme: https://yellowcard.mhra.gov.uk/

**Canada:**
- Health Canada: https://www.canada.ca/en/health-canada/services/drugs-health-products/medeffect-canada.html

**Australia:**
- Therapeutic Goods Administration: https://www.tga.gov.au/

**Other Countries:**
- Contact your national health authority or medicines regulatory agency
